This Power BI dashboard provides a detailed analysis of 817,860 in total pizza sales and 21,350 orders, uncovering patterns in customer preferences, sales trends, and product performance. Through interactive visualizations, it highlights revenue drivers, peak sales periods, and top-performing pizza categories, enabling restaurants to make data-driven decisions to boost growth.

The analysis shows that an average of 138 pizzas are sold per day, with sales peaking in the afternoon (24K pizzas) and on Fridays (8.1K orders), suggesting strong weekend and midday demand. Classic pizzas lead the market with 220K sales, followed by Supreme, Chicken, and Veggie categories.

Monthly trends reveal fluctuations, with sales performing strongly in July and March, while dipping in September and October. These seasonal variations highlight opportunities for targeted promotions during low-demand months.

Product-level insights indicate that pizzas like Barbecue Chicken (+18.5%), Big Meat (+16.5%), and Classic Deluxe (+17.1%) show consistent growth, while others such as Calabrese Pizza (-24.1%) are declining in demand. This differentiation helps businesses optimize inventory, focus marketing on high performers, and rethink underperforming products.

Customer demand analysis suggests that focusing resources on afternoon operations, weekend marketing, and promoting popular pizzas can maximize sales. In addition, expanding variety in fast-growing categories like Chicken and Deluxe pizzas can further strengthen revenue streams.

This dashboard demonstrates how data-driven insights can transform restaurant operations by identifying growth opportunities, improving customer satisfaction, and refining menu strategies.
